The harvesting phase is critical as it directly influences the fruit's post-harvest life and quality.


Maturity Indexing for Non-Climacteric Fruit:


  • Optimal Timing: Dragon fruit is harvested at peak ripeness—30–32 days after flowering—by expert teams ensuring vibrant color and full size
  • Visual & Sensory Assessment: Each fruit is carefully checked for 70% uniform color, firmness, and slightly softened bracts

Gentle Harvesting Technique:


  • Sterilized Tools: Harvesters use sterilized tools to cut the fruit, leaving 1–2 cm of stem attached to prevent rot and extend shelf life.
  • Temperature-Controlled Harvesting: Harvesting is done in the cooler early mornings or late afternoons to reduce field heat and slow post-harvest decay.
  • Immediate Field Protection: Harvested fruits are promptly placed in clean, padded, ventilated crates, avoiding sun, dust, and contaminants to prevent bruising or damage.
